About

Researcher. Programmer. Teacher

My B.S. is in Physics and Mathematics (University of Wisconsin-Madison 2018), and my PhD is in Materials Science (University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill 2024) supervised by Daphne Klotsa with a Graduate Certificate in Innovation, Leadership, and Management.

Throughout my undergraduate career, I developed computational models in Python and IDL to simulate and predict the energy emissions of fusion-plasma generators, which I applied to design, fabricate, and experimentally verify a x-ray detector for my senior thesis.

For my PhD thesis, I am utilizing C++ to computationally model large systems of self-propelled particles and Python to measure the dynamic properties, statistically analyze big data for trends, and create intuitive visualizations to explain the significance. Furthermore, I derive mathematical theories based upon fundamental physics that explain and predict the complex collective behavior observed in active systems.

As a teaching assistant, I developed a Tableau front-end system to visualize and analyze user analytics in real-time with a back-end database connected and maintained with SQL. My analyses and visualizations have been used in grant proposals to increase funding, for scheduling purposes to reduce extraneous costs, and in marketing materials to increase user diversity.

Finally, I am developing projects on the side using HTML, JavaScript, CSS, SQL, C++, and machine learning to hone my skills as a data scientist and software developer.
